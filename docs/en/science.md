# Science Background & Honest Clarification

[中文版](../zh/science.md)

## What Are Binaural Beats?

Binaural beats are an auditory phenomenon: when each ear receives a pure tone at a slightly different frequency (e.g., left ear 395 Hz, right ear 405 Hz), the brain perceives a rhythmic pulsation corresponding to the frequency difference (10 Hz in this example). This pulsation **does not exist in the physical sound wave** — it is a product of neural phase-difference encoding in the Superior Olivary Complex of the brainstem.

## ✅ Scientifically Confirmed Facts

- **Binaural beats are a real auditory-neural phenomenon.** Since Heinrich Wilhelm Dove first described them in 1839, extensive electrophysiological research has confirmed the brainstem's phase-locked response to interaural frequency differences (Licklider, 1948; Wernick & Starr, 1968).
- **The Frequency Following Response (FFR) can be objectively measured.** EEG can record steady-state evoked potentials synchronized with the beat frequency — this is not subjective speculation (Pratt et al., 2010; Gao et al., 2014).
- **Frequency following has an upper limit.** When the beat frequency exceeds approximately 30–35 Hz, the brainstem's phase-locking ability drops sharply (Licklider, 1948), and beat perception collapses into two separate tones — the "Collapse Zone" preset in this tool lets you experience this boundary firsthand.

## ⚠️ What Commercial Marketing Has Exaggerated

Many binaural beat products claim to "boost intelligence," "replace meditation," "treat insomnia/anxiety/ADHD," or even "activate the pineal gland." **A critical perspective is needed:**

1. **Brainstem FFR ≠ cortical brainwave entrainment.** The frequency following response produced by binaural beats in the brainstem does not mean the entire cerebral cortex is "entrained" to the same frequency. This is a level-of-analysis error. There is currently a lack of strong evidence that listening to binaural beats can reliably alter dominant cortical brainwave bands (Orozco Perez et al., 2020; Garcia-Argibay et al., 2019).

2. **Placebo and expectancy effects are significant.** Multiple double-blind controlled studies have found that subjects report greater relaxation when told "this is a relaxation frequency," regardless of whether the audio actually contains binaural beats (Wahbeh et al., 2007). **Your subjective experience likely stems from the relaxation context itself (eyes closed, quiet environment, headphones isolating external noise), not from any specific effect of binaural beats.**

3. **No authoritative medical institution lists binaural beats as a treatment.** FDA, WHO, and NIH clinical guidelines do not include binaural beat therapy. Using them to treat anxiety, insomnia, or attention disorders lacks evidence-based support.

4. **"Delta waves for sleep, Beta waves for focus" is an oversimplification.** The relationship between brainwave frequency bands and states of consciousness is far more complex than popular science descriptions suggest — it is not a simple one-to-one mapping.

## 🔬 Scientific Exploration Value of This Tool

Despite the lack of support for therapeutic claims, binaural beats have clear value as a **psychoacoustics and neuroscience teaching demonstration tool:**

- **Experience the nonlinear properties of the auditory system:** your brain "hears" a beat that doesn't exist in the physical world — an intuitive demonstration that perception ≠ physical input.
- **Explore the boundaries of frequency following:** personally experiencing the disappearance of the beat at 30+ Hz difference is more intuitive than any textbook description.
- **Understand SSB frequency shifting:** the single-sideband modulation used in music mode is a classic communications engineering technique, made audible here.
- **Subjective experience of beat perception:** different people may perceive the same parameters differently — this itself is an interesting psychoacoustics research topic.

**This tool is an experimental exploration tool, not a medical device. No therapeutic claims are made.**
